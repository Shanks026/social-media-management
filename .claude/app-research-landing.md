# Tercero — Landing Page Brief for Stitch AI

> **Purpose**: Product and design brief for building a marketing landing page for Tercero. Use this document to understand what the product does, who it's for, what features to highlight, how it should look, and what copy direction to follow.

---

## 1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW

**Product name**: Tercero (pronounced "ter-SAY-ro" — Spanish for "third", as in the third member of your team)

**One-line pitch**: The all-in-one operations platform for social media agencies.

**Expanded pitch**: Tercero replaces the fragmented stack of Notion, spreadsheets, approval emails, and accounting tools that agencies use to manage clients. It gives agency owners a single workspace to run their entire business — from chasing leads to delivering content to getting paid.

**Target user**: Social media agency owners and managers handling 3–30+ clients. They're not developers — they're operators who value speed, clarity, and professionalism.

**Core problem solved**: Agencies juggle too many tools. Client briefs in one place, post drafts in another, approvals over WhatsApp, invoices in Excel, tasks in Notion. Tercero collapses all of this into one structured, professional workspace.

---

## 2. KEY VALUE PROPOSITIONS

These are the "why us" points — lead with these on the landing page.

### 1. Full-funnel, from lead to invoice
Tercero covers the entire agency lifecycle in one tool:
> Prospect a lead → Send a proposal → Win the deal → Convert to client → Create content → Get client approval → Invoice → Track payment

No switching apps between stages.

### 2. Content approvals without the back-and-forth
Clients review and approve posts via a shareable link — **no login required**, no new account to create. They see a clean, branded review page and click Approve or Request Revisions. The agency sees the update instantly.

### 3. Every client's pipeline at a glance
The dashboard shows every client's content health in one screen — who's overdue, who's stuck in drafts, what's scheduled. No digging through folders or chasing teammates for status updates.

### 4. Content versioning built in
Every post revision is saved. When a client requests changes, a new version is created automatically. The full history — what changed, when, and what the client said — is always accessible.

### 5. Finances connected to client work
Invoice clients, track expenses, and see profit-per-client — all tied to the same client records. No re-entering data between tools.

### 6. Built for agencies, not solo freelancers
Multi-client architecture from the ground up. Team invites. An internal workspace for the agency's own social media. Client-specific branding on approval portals (higher plans).

## 3. FEATURES — LANDING PAGE SUMMARY

Use these as feature section copy. Listed in recommended highlight priority.

---

### Client Management
A full database of every client — their branding, platforms, billing, content pipeline, and contact details all in one profile. Client cards show urgency status at a glance: who has overdue posts, who's healthy, who's idle.

- Industry and tier tagging (VIP, PRO)
- Social platform links per client
- Per-client financial metrics (MRR, profit margin)
- 8-tab client detail view: content, billing, campaigns, proposals, documents, calendar, and more

---

### Content Management & Approval Workflow
Draft posts, get them approved, schedule them — all in one place. Supports Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, YouTube, Facebook, and Google Business.

- Platform-specific post previews (Instagram feed, LinkedIn post, tweet card, YouTube thumbnail)
- Multi-version history: every revision saved, nothing lost
- Send for client approval with one click
- Client approves via shareable link — no login needed
- Revision feedback captured and linked to the new version
- Status pipeline: Draft → Pending Approval → Needs Revision → Scheduled → Published

---

### Content Calendar
Visual calendar view of all scheduled content across all clients.

- Month and week views
- Color-coded by status and platform
- Click any date to create a post pre-filled with that date
- Filter by client, platform, and status
- Export a PDF content report (Velocity+ plans)

---

### Campaigns
Group related posts into named initiatives with analytics, budget tracking, and a single client review link for the whole campaign. Available on Velocity and Quantum plans.

- KPI bar: total posts, published, on-time rate, average approval time
- Platform distribution chart
- Budget vs. invoiced vs. collected tracker
- Single campaign review link — client can approve or request revisions on all posts in one session

---

### Prospects CRM
A built-in lead pipeline so agencies never lose track of a potential client.

- 7-stage pipeline: New → Contacted → Follow-Up → Demo Scheduled → Proposal Sent → Won → Lost
- Log every outreach: calls, emails, DMs, meetings, notes
- Import leads in bulk from Apollo, Apify, Google Maps, or any CSV
- Automatic activity log when a prospect's status changes
- Link proposals and documents directly to a prospect
- One-click Convert to Client — all data carries over, nothing re-entered

---

### Proposals
Send professional proposals that prospects can accept or decline online — no PDF email attachments, no chasing for a response.

- Build proposals inside Tercero with live preview, or upload an existing PDF
- Shareable public link — prospect opens it, reads it, clicks Accept or Decline
- View tracking: know when they opened it
- Full status timeline: Draft → Sent → Viewed → Accepted / Declined
- PDF export for built proposals
- Proposal limit per plan (Trial/Ignite: 5; Velocity/Quantum: unlimited)

---

### Finance
Invoice clients, track expenses, and watch profit margins — all connected to the client data already in Tercero.

- Create invoices with unlimited line items; auto-numbered (INV-YYYY-###)
- Status workflow: Draft → Sent → Overdue → Paid
- PDF invoice export with agency branding
- Mark as Paid → automatically creates an income entry in the ledger
- Recurring invoice templates for retainer billing (Velocity+)
- Expense subscriptions: track tool costs, assign to clients (Velocity+)
- Transaction ledger: full income and expense history
- Finance overview: revenue vs. expenses chart, outstanding invoice KPI, net profit

---

### Documents
Centralised file storage for every client — contracts, briefs, brand kits, reports.

- Upload and categorise files per client
- Secure signed URLs (private storage — no public links)
- Organise into named collections (Velocity+)
- 20 GB storage on Ignite, 100 GB on Velocity, 500 GB on Quantum

---

### Meetings & Notes
Keep track of every client call and every internal task without switching to Notion or Asana.

- Schedule meetings with dates, times, links (Zoom/Meet), and notes
- Task/reminder system with due dates, client assignments, and status (To Do / Done / Archived)
- Notes grouped as: Overdue → Upcoming → Done → Archived
- In-app meeting reminders (no external push required)

---

### Team Collaboration
Invite teammates to the agency workspace. Every team member has access to all clients, content, and finance data scoped to the agency.

- Generate invite links; teammates create their own account
- Role-based system (admin owner + team members)
- Live updates via Supabase Realtime

---

### White-label Client Portals
Higher plan agencies can brand the client-facing review pages with their own logo — removing all Tercero branding for a professional, seamless client experience.

- Ignite: Tercero branding on all portals
- Velocity: Agency logo on all portals ("Powered by Tercero" footer)
- Quantum: Full white-label — agency logo only, no Tercero mention anywhere

## 7. DESIGN SYSTEM & AESTHETICS

Use these as direct design direction for Stitch AI when building the landing page.

### Overall Aesthetic
- **Style**: Clean, modern, minimal. Confident and professional — not playful or startup-casual.
- **Feel**: Dense information delivered with clarity. Like a well-designed SaaS dashboard, not a marketing brochure.
- **Inspiration**: Linear, Vercel, Clerk — dark-mode-first, neutral palette, precision spacing.

### Color Palette

The app uses a **neutral / achromatic** palette — no brand accent color. Black, white, and greys do all the work. Semantic colors (red for destructive, green for success, amber for warning) are used sparingly for status indicators only.

**Light mode:**
- Background: `#ffffff` (pure white)
- Foreground / text: near-black (`oklch(0.145 0 0)` ≈ `#1a1a1a`)
- Card surface: `#ffffff`
- Muted text: medium grey (`oklch(0.556 0 0)` ≈ `#737373`)
- Border: light grey (`oklch(0.922 0 0)` ≈ `#ebebeb`)
- Secondary / muted surface: near-white (`oklch(0.97 0 0)` ≈ `#f7f7f7`)

**Dark mode (primary aesthetic for landing page):**
- Background: near-black (`oklch(0.145 0 0)` ≈ `#1a1a1a`)
- Foreground / text: near-white (`oklch(0.985 0 0)` ≈ `#f9f9f9`)
- Card surface: dark grey (`oklch(0.205 0 0)` ≈ `#2b2b2b`)
- Muted text: mid grey (`oklch(0.708 0 0)` ≈ `#a3a3a3`)
- Border: subtle white overlay (`oklch(1 0 0 / 10%)`)
- Secondary surface: slightly lighter dark (`oklch(0.269 0 0)` ≈ `#3d3d3d`)
- Sidebar: `oklch(0.205 0 0)` ≈ `#2b2b2b`

**Accent color for landing page CTAs** (from dark mode chart palette — used for interactive highlights):
- Primary accent: blue-purple (`oklch(0.488 0.243 264.376)` ≈ `#4f46e5` indigo)
- Supporting: teal-green (`oklch(0.696 0.17 162.48)` ≈ `#34d399`)

### Typography

**Primary font**: **Google Sans** (loaded from Google Fonts)
- Variable optical sizing font; clean, geometric, professional
- Used for all UI text in the app
- Fallback stack: `system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif`

**Secondary font available**: Poppins (available but not active in current build — can be used for large display headings on the landing page if desired)

**Type scale guidance**:
- Hero headline: 56–72px, weight 700, tight tracking
- Section headlines: 32–40px, weight 600
- Body / feature copy: 16–18px, weight 400
- Labels / captions: 13–14px, weight 500, muted color

### Shape & Spacing

- **Border radius**: `0.625rem` (10px) as base — scales up to `calc(radius + 16px)` (26px) for cards and panels
- Cards feel rounded but not bubbly — restrained, professional
- Consistent `24px` section padding inside content areas (`p-6`)
- Clean gutters; nothing cramped

### Component Style (shadcn/ui New York variant)

- Cards: flat surface, thin border, no heavy shadows — depth comes from background contrast
- Buttons: filled primary (dark/black on light, white on dark), ghost and outline variants for secondary actions
- Badges: small, rounded-full pills — color-coded by status or category
- Inputs: minimal, border-only, no heavy box shadows
- Dialogs/Modals: centered, 600–800px max width, clean close button, no heavy backdrop blur

### Icons

Lucide React icons exclusively — thin stroke, consistent 16–20px sizing. No filled icons, no emoji in UI chrome (emoji used only for empty states and decorative moments).

### Motion & Animation

- Page transitions: `opacity 0 → 1` fade-in over 500ms ease
- Shimmer/spotlight sweep animation on loading text (1.8s ease-in-out infinite)
- Button loading states: spinner inside button, no layout shift
- No heavy parallax or scroll-jacking — motion is subtle and purposeful

### Scrollbar

- Thin (6px), transparent track, semi-transparent thumb that appears on hover
- Pill-shaped (`border-radius: 9999px`)
- Subtle — doesn't distract from content

## 8. TONE & VOICE

- **Confident and direct.** No fluff, no filler adjectives.
- **Operator-focused.** The audience runs businesses. Speak to efficiency and control, not excitement.
- **No hype.** Avoid words like "revolutionary", "game-changing", "supercharge". Say what the feature does, not how amazing it is.
- **Short sentences.** If a sentence can be split, split it.
- **Active voice.** "Clients approve content via a link" — not "Content can be approved by clients through a shared link."

**Example headline styles that fit the brand:**
- "Run your agency. Not your inbox."
- "Content, clients, invoices — one place."
- "Your clients approve posts without creating an account."
- "From lead to invoice, without switching tabs."

**Example styles to avoid:**
- "Supercharge your agency workflow with AI-powered tools!"
- "The #1 platform for social media agencies 🚀"
- "We help you do more with less."

## 9. LANDING PAGE SECTION RECOMMENDATIONS

Suggested section order for the landing page:

1. **Hero** — headline + sub-headline + primary CTA (Start Free Trial) + secondary CTA (See Features) + hero visual (dashboard screenshot or mockup)
2. **Social proof bar** — logos or a short stat strip ("Used by X agencies", etc.)
3. **Problem statement** — brief "you're juggling too many tools" section
4. **Feature overview** — 3-column icon grid of the main modules
5. **Deep-dive sections** — alternate left/right layout for: Content Approval → Proposals → Prospects CRM → Finance → Campaigns
6. **Pricing table** — 3 plans (Ignite / Velocity / Quantum) with feature comparison
7. **Platform logos** — Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc.
8. **Final CTA block** — "Start your free 14-day trial"
9. **Footer** — links, legal, social

## 11. WHAT NOT TO INCLUDE ON THE LANDING PAGE

- No mention of the tech stack (React, Supabase, etc.) — irrelevant to the target audience
- No mention of INR pricing being manual/contact-based unless directly asked
- No mention of trial limitations unless shown as a plan comparison (keep it positive)
- No mention of "Internal Account" or workspace architecture — this is an advanced concept for onboarding, not marketing
